Your choice of signer determines how users authenticate. The right one depends on who your users are and whether they already have a wallet.

## Native signers

Native signers are cryptographic primitives built directly into the Rhinestone SDK — no third-party provider required.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="ECDSA" icon="key" href="./ecdsa-signer">
    Standard Ethereum key pairs. Supports single-owner and multisig (n/m threshold) via the Ownable Validator. Best for server-side and agent wallets.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Passkey" icon="fingerprint" href="./passkeys">
    WebAuthn-based biometric auth using the device secure enclave (Face ID, Touch ID). No seed phrases. Supports single-owner and multisig (n/m threshold). Best for consumer apps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-factor" icon="shield-check" href="../advanced/multi-factor-authentication">
    Combine multiple validators in tandem — e.g. require both an ECDSA key and a passkey to sign. Best for high-security accounts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Embedded wallet signers

Embedded wallets are generated and managed inside your app. Users authenticate via social login, email, or biometrics — no browser extension or seed phrase required. Keys are protected in a secure enclave (TEE), so neither you nor the provider ever sees the full key.

Use these when you're onboarding users who don't already have a crypto wallet.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Privy" icon="shield" href="./signers/privy">
    Social and email login with embedded wallets. Most popular for consumer apps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dynamic" icon="bolt" href="./signers/dynamic">
    Multi-chain embedded wallets with social and email login.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Para" icon="globe" href="./signers/para">
    Embedded wallets with MPC key management.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Magic" icon="wand-magic" href="./signers/magic">
    Email and social login with non-custodial embedded wallets.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Turnkey" icon="server" href="./signers/turnkey">
    Programmatic wallet infrastructure. Good for server-side and agent use cases.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Openfort" icon="gamepad" href="./signers/openfort">
    Embedded wallets with a gaming-first developer experience.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## External wallet signers

External wallets are browser extensions or mobile apps that users already own and hold keys to — MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Rabby, and any WalletConnect-compatible wallet.

Use these when you're building a dApp for existing Web3 users who want to bring their own wallet.
